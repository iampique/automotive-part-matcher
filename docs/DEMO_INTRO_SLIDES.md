# 3 Slides: Set Up the Demo

---

## Slide 1

**Title:** Today: days from “customer sent requirements” to “we have a shortlist to quote”

**On the slide (3 bullets):**
- Customer sends an RFQ or requirement doc—often long, mixed formats, prose.
- Someone has to read it, pull out the real specs (pin count, voltage, environment, certs, lead time), then search the catalog.
- That manual step is where the delay is. The bottleneck isn’t finding the part; it’s getting from the document to a clear, structured “what we’re looking for.”

**What you say:**  
“So the slow part isn’t the catalog or the quote engine. It’s the step in the middle: turning what the customer wrote into something we can actually match against. That’s manual, and it takes days.”

---

## Slide 2

**Title:** What we built: requirement doc in → matched shortlist out

**On the slide (3 bullets):**
- You give the system the requirement (paste text or upload the doc). No forms, no templates.
- It reads it, extracts the specs that matter, and matches against the connector catalog.
- You get a ranked shortlist with part numbers and a clear “why this fits” for each—in one step.

**What you say:**  
“So instead of someone spending days reading and extracting, we put the document in and get a shortlist with explanations back. Same input customers send today; the extraction and matching happen in one pass.”

---

## Slide 3

**Title:** What you’ll see in the next 5 minutes

**On the slide (3 bullets):**
- I’ll use real requirement text—or upload a doc like the ones we get from customers.
- The system will return ranked connector matches with a fit score and a short explanation for each.
- Watch for: how fast it runs, how the “why it fits” lines up with what’s in the requirement, and that we’re not filling out forms—we’re feeding the doc.

**What you say:**  
“In the demo I’ll paste or upload a requirement, hit search, and we’ll see the shortlist and explanations. Look for speed, relevance of the matches, and that the reasoning is grounded in what was in the document. Then we can talk about rolling this out.”

---

**Handoff to demo:**  
“Here’s the app. I’ll paste [or upload] a requirement and run it.”
