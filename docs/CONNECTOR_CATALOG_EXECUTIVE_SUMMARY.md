# Connector Catalog: Executive Summary

**What it is**  
The connector catalog is our **master list of electrical connectors** we can quote and supply. Each part has a part number, a short description, technical specs (pin count, voltage, current, temperature range, environmental rating), certifications (e.g. IATF 16949, RoHS, AEC-Q200), typical applications (e.g. Infotainment, Battery Management, ADAS), and commercial terms (unit price, lead time).

**Why it matters**  
When a customer sends an RFQ or requirement document, we have to figure out *which* of these parts fit. Today that’s manual: someone reads the requirement, pulls out the key specs, and searches or filters the catalog. The demo system does that step automatically: it takes the requirement (text or document), interprets what the customer needs, and returns a **ranked shortlist of catalog parts** that match, with a brief “why it fits” for each. So the catalog is the **single source of truth** we match against—same parts we’d quote today, but found in seconds instead of days.

**In one sentence**  
The connector catalog is our quotable product set; the system uses it to turn a customer’s requirement into a shortlist of parts we can propose and price.
